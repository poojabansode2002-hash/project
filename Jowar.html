What is Jowar

Jowar is a cereal plant and has been classified as one of the major grains grown in the world. The first sorghum species has been found in Australia, Africa, the Indian Ocean, Mesoamerica, and parts of the Pacific Ocean. Jowar grows well in hot climates. Jowar is one of the most important cereals in the world. It is the leading food for millions of poor people living in rural areas of Asia and Africa.

Jowar is grown for domestic consumption and as a cash crop. It has several uses, including human food, animal feed, alcoholic beverages, and biofuels. Nutritionists classify sorghum as very healthy because it is rich in essential nutrients for the body. Due to the growing nutritional and commercial value, many regions of the world produce Jowar on a large scale. According to the statistics data from the Food and Agriculture Organization (FAOSTAT), the United States is the largest producer of Jowar in the world, with 11.5 million tonnes of total production.

Fertilizers required : NPK fertilizer with at least six compounds of the nutrients, S, mg, and zn, urea

pesticides required : earhead bug, calocoris, clothianidin, imidaclo-prid
